## Understanding Scholarship Information

Each scholarship listing in the app displays key information to help you identify relevant opportunities:

<Accordion title="Convocatoria (Call for Applications)">
  The name and title of the scholarship program or opportunity. This identifies the specific program, institution, or organization offering the scholarship.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Beneficiados (Eligible Students)">
  Indicates which students can apply, such as:
  - All undergraduate programs (Todas las carreras)
  - All graduate programs (Todos los posgrados)
  - Specific degree programs
  - CUCEI community members
  - Recent graduates (Egresados, Egresadas)
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Fecha (Application Period)">
  The date range during which applications are accepted. Pay close attention to these dates as many scholarships have strict deadlines.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Resumen (Summary)">
  A brief description of the program's purpose, target audience, and key benefits. This helps you quickly determine if a scholarship matches your interests and qualifications.
</Accordion>

The app provides access to information about SUBES, including:

## Application Process

When you find a scholarship that interests you:

1. **Review Requirements** - Tap the "Más información" button to visit the official scholarship page
2. **Check Eligibility** - Verify you meet all criteria including academic requirements
3. **Prepare Documents** - Gather necessary documentation early
4. **Meet Deadlines** - Submit applications before the closing date
5. **Follow Up** - Check official results through the Coordination of Internationalization website

<Accordion title="Common Requirements">
  Most scholarships require:
  - Active student status at UdeG
  - Minimum GPA (often 8.0 or higher)
  - Completed percentage of degree credits (usually 60% minimum)
  - Academic transcript
  - Letter of motivation or intent
  - Proof of enrollment
</Accordion>
