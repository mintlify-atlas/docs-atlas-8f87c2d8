## Social Service Overview

Social service (Servicio Social) is a mandatory requirement for graduation at CUCEI. It provides an opportunity to apply your academic knowledge while contributing to community wellbeing. This program fosters social responsibility and professional development through practical, supervised experiences.

<Note>
Social service is more than a graduation requirement - it's an opportunity to make a real difference while developing professional skills and expanding your network.
</Note>

## Program Requirements

To participate in social service, you must meet specific eligibility criteria.

### Eligibility Requirements

<Accordion title="Enrollment Status">
  You must be currently enrolled (matriculado) as an active student at the institution. Students who have taken a leave of absence or are not currently registered typically cannot participate.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Academic Progress">
  You must have completed at least 60% of your degree program credits. This ensures you have sufficient knowledge and skills to contribute meaningfully during your service.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Grade Point Average">
  A minimum GPA of 8.0 (on a 10-point scale) is required to be eligible for social service participation. Maintain good academic standing throughout your studies.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Formal Application">
  Submit a complete and properly formatted formal application (solicitud formal) according to the instructions provided by your coordinator.
</Accordion>

### Required Documentation

When applying for social service, prepare:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Student Credential" icon="id-card">

  </Card>
  <Card title="Motivation Letter" icon="pen-to-square">

  </Card>

## Application Process

Follow these steps to successfully apply for social service:

### Step 1: Review Requirements

- Verify you meet all eligibility criteria
- Check your credit completion percentage
- Confirm your GPA meets the minimum
- Review required documentation list

### Step 2: Submit Application

<Accordion title="Online Submission">
  Many coordinators accept online applications:
  - Access the designated platform
  - Complete all required fields accurately
  - Upload required documents in specified formats
  - Submit before the deadline
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="In-Person Submission">
  Some programs require physical submission:
  - Print completed application form
  - Gather all physical document copies
  - Submit during office hours
  - Obtain receipt or confirmation
</Accordion>

### Step 3: Evaluation and Assignment

1. **Application Review**
   - Coordinator reviews your submission
   - Documents are verified for completeness
   - Eligibility is confirmed

2. **Tutor Assignment**
   - You're assigned an academic supervisor
   - Tutor will guide you through the process
   - Maintain regular communication with your tutor

3. **Program Placement**
   - You may be matched with a specific project
   - Consider your interests and schedule
   - Location and responsibilities are determined

### Step 4: Orientation

<Note>
Attend the initial orientation session. This is mandatory and provides essential information for your social service experience.
</Note>

During orientation you'll learn about:
- Program expectations and responsibilities
- Documentation requirements throughout
- Evaluation criteria and reporting schedules
- Safety protocols and professional conduct
- Contact information for support

## Completing Your Service

### Final Requirements

To successfully complete social service:

<Accordion title="Hour Completion">
  Fulfill all 480 required hours with proper documentation and verification from your service site.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Final Report">
  Submit a comprehensive final report describing your activities, learning outcomes, and reflections on the experience.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Supervisor Evaluation">
  Receive satisfactory evaluation from your site supervisor confirming your participation and performance.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Tutor Approval">
  Obtain final approval from your academic tutor confirming all requirements are met.
</Accordion>

### Official Release Letter

Upon successful completion:
- You'll receive an official social service release letter
- This document is required for graduation
- Keep multiple copies for your records
- Submit to Control Escolar as directed

<Note>
The release letter (carta de liberación) is an essential graduation document. Protect it carefully and make copies immediately upon receipt.
</Note>

## Frequently Asked Questions

<Accordion title="Can I complete social service before reaching 60% credit completion?">
  No, you must have completed at least 60% of your degree credits before beginning social service. This ensures you have adequate knowledge to contribute meaningfully.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I do social service at my workplace?">
  Possibly, if your workplace qualifies as an approved social service site and your activities meet program requirements. Consult with your coordinator about approval process.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What if I can't complete the hours by the deadline?">
  Contact your tutor immediately if you're falling behind. Extensions may be possible with valid justification, but don't wait until the last minute to ask.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Is social service paid?">
  Some placements offer stipends or support, but social service is primarily a volunteer educational requirement. Focus on the learning and networking benefits.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I change my placement if it's not a good fit?">
  In some cases yes, but work with your tutor to resolve issues first. Frequent changes can delay your completion and may not be permitted.
</Accordion>
