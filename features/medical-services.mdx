## Medical Documentation for Absences

If you need to justify class absences due to medical reasons, university medical services can help.

### Getting Medical Documentation

1. **Visit a University Clinic**
   - Schedule or walk into an appropriate clinic
   - Consult with a healthcare provider
   - Explain your need for medical documentation

2. **IMSS Services**
   - IMSS (Instituto Mexicano del Seguro Social) documentation is also accepted
   - IMSS disability certificates may provide more extensive coverage

3. **Submit to Your Coordinator**
   - Take medical documentation to your academic coordinator
   - Follow the [absence justification process](/features/academic-services#absence-justification)

<Accordion title="What Documentation Should Include">
  Medical documentation for absence justification should specify:
  - Date of medical consultation
  - Specific dates requiring absence from classes
  - Healthcare provider's signature and credentials
  - Institution stamp or official marking
</Accordion>

## International Students

### Special Considerations

International students should be aware of:

<Accordion title="Health Insurance Requirements">
  - Many exchange programs require proof of health insurance
  - Verify coverage includes care in Mexico
  - Understand what services are covered
  - Know how to file claims if needed
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Language Support">
  - Bring a Spanish-speaking friend if language is a barrier
  - Some clinics may have English-speaking staff
  - Translation apps can help with basic communication
  - Student services may provide interpretation assistance
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Cultural Differences">
  - Healthcare practices may differ from your home country
  - Ask questions if you don't understand procedures
  - Prescriptions and medications may have different names
  - Payment and insurance processes might be unfamiliar
</Accordion>

### When to Use Medical Services

**Visit University Clinics For**:
- Routine checkups and preventive care
- Minor illnesses (colds, flu, infections)
- Mental health support and counseling
- Dental care and oral health
- Health education and advice
- Medical documentation for absences

**Seek Emergency Care For**:
- Severe injuries
- Chest pain or difficulty breathing
- Loss of consciousness
- Severe bleeding
- Suspected fractures
- Any life-threatening condition

Your health is essential to your academic success. Don't hesitate to use available medical services when you need them.
